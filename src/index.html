code

Almost all the basic principles of programming are followed, to name few are KISS (Keep It Simple, Stupid), DRY (Don’t Repeat Yourself), YAGNI ( You Aren’t Gonna Need It) and SOC (Separation Of Concern) are employed while developing the application. At least 10-20 percent of my project are based in TDD (Test Driven Development) and ensure the codes are maintainable for future features). Primary focus starts with MVP (Minimum Viable Product) and build up on it to make performance and experience better.

optimization

Google’s PageSpeed Insight and Google’s Lighthouse are my favorite tools when it comes to performance optimization, some of other tools I use are GTmetrix and Uptrends. Periodic testing ensures future optimization is done without affecting the running application and making it better. Webpack keeps on me on track at the time development as well as for performance enhancement, image srcset, SVG sprites, lazy loading and code splitting, Gzipping and minification are some of the techniques while developing. I keep the security part of the application in the optimization section for my own reasons.

design

All the aspects of Usefulness, Usability and Value of the application is defined here. I follow Jesse James Garrett’s process of 5 planes i.e. Strategy, Scope, Structure, Skeleton and Surface. To me the Flow is everything and user’s doesn’t have to think twice before using the application and achieve their goal with ease. I think I apply at least 30% of the way how Steve Jobs used to think about connecting the product to the end user with perfection and ease and I am working on other 70% :)

experience

Google Analytics is what I used for monitoring user engagement and experience and client goal with the application. I have certifications in Google Analytics and Google Adwords. This is whole different field of study but I keep all the basic rules and techniques in mind to ensure application meets the user’s need. This part relates to the previous to some extent as flow of UI and UX plays major role in customer experience.
